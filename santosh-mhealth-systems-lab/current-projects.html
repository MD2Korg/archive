Research Projects

 

Active Grants

1R24EB025845-01 (Sim) Kumar (Co-I) 8/1/17 – 7/31/20 National Institutes of Health, NIBIB Open mHealth: Community-Based Data and Metadata Standards for Mobile Health The goal of this project is the development, test and validation, repository and dissemination of a software infrastructure that addresses data FAIR (findable, accessible, interoperable and reusable) using data and metadata standards.

IIS-1722646 (Marlin) Kumar (Co-PI) 10/1/17 – 9/30/20 National Science Foundation SCH: INT: Collaborative Research: Enhancing Context-Awareness and Personalization for Intensively Adaptive Smoking Cessation Messaging Interventions The goals of this project are to adapt computational models for detecting stress, craving, smoking, eating and geoexposures from mobile sensor data by incorporating modeling advances; determine timing for intervention and adaptation of content based on detection of the above listed biomarkers; and to facilitate intervention data collection for user students.

ACI-1640813 Kumar (PI) 9/1/16 – 8/31/21 National Science Foundation, ACI CIF21 DIBBs: EI: mProv: Provence-Based Data Analytics Cyberinfrastructure for High-frequency Mobile Sensor Data The goal of this project is develop a metadata (provenance) cyberinfrastructure to facilitate sharing of high-frequency mobile sensor data for third-party research.

R01MD010362 (Wetter) Kumar (Co-I) 3/3/16 – 1/31/21 National Institutes of Health, NCI Eliminating Tobacco-Related Disparities among African American Smokers The proposed study will investigate the processes and mechanisms underlying smoking cessation among African American smokers attempting to quit.

1R01DE025244 (Shetty) Kumar (Co-I) 7/1/15 – 5/31/20 National Institutes of Health, NIDCR ROBAS: A multimodal Sensor System for Remote Assessment of Oral Health Behavior The goal of this project is to develop a user-centric, multimodal sensing system that can provide objective, individual-level and ecologically-valid data on oral hygiene behaviors.

1R01CA190329 (Wetter) Kumar (Co-I) 7/1/15 – 6/30/20 National Institutes of Health, NCI Socioeconomic Status, Stress, and Smoking Cessation The goal of this project is to examine the influence of SES and social history, contextual and environmental influences, biobehavioral/psychosocial predispositions, and acute momentary precipitants on stress, smoking lapse, and abstinence among smokers attempting to quit.

1U54EB020404 Kumar (PI) 9/29/14 – 9/30/19 National Institutes of Health – BD2K Initiative Center of Excellence for Mobile Sensor Data-to-Knowledge (MD2K) This project is developing novel big data solutions to reliably quantify physical, biological, behavioral, social and environmental factors that contribute to health and disease risk in the context of two complex health conditions with high mortality risk – reducing hospital readmission in congestive heart failure (CHF) patients and preventing relapse in abstinent smokers, both via sensor-triggered mHealth interventions.

CNS-1823221 Kumar (PI) 10/1/18 – 9/30/21 National Science Foundation CRI: CI-EN” Collaborative Research: mResearch: A platform for reproducible and extensible mobile sensor big data research The goal of the proposed mResearch project is to make necessary enhancements to the MD2K infrastructure so it becomes a true community resource that computing researchers from across the country and around the world can easily use to conduct reproducible and extensible research on any aspect of the collection and analysis of high-frequency mobile sensor data.

1R01CA224537-01 (Wetter) Kumar (Co-I) 1/19/18 – 12/31/22 National Institutes of Health Affective Science and Smoking Cessation: Real Time World Assessment The goals of this project are the analysis of the physiological sensor data and self-report data collected in the study, and the deployment of ongoing conceptual and technological advances using continuous measure of stress obtained from AutoSense to examine and relate critical components of stress response measures to behavioral risk factors representing common adherence challenges such as smoking cessation and dietary modification in diverse populations.

1U01CA229437-01 (Nahum-Shani) Kumar (Co-I) 9/1/18 – 8/31/22 National Institutes of Health Novel use of mHealth Data to Identify states of vulnerability and receptivity to JITAIs The goal of this project is to apply innovative computational approaches to one of the most extensive and racially/ethnically diverse collection of real time, real world data on health behavior change (smoking cessation) to inform the development of theory-driven behavioral interventions.
